Rush Hour

Event Details.

This event consists of 2 rounds
Round 1 : The Event begins in a room, where each team will be handed over with chits notifying them what they should be looking for. Each team will look for the particular balloon, bottle, pouch or in the junk box and try to find another chit containing a program code. As soon as they find the code, they will be asked to rush to the lab where computers will be set for them.
*The teams will also have an extra option in the room called-Try your luck where instead of looking for what asked, they can solve a sudoku puzzle and get the program code. Only the team which asks first can use this option, and once they start the puzzle they will not be allowed to go back to searching.
Round 2 : The round 2 begins in the lab. Participants will just have to type the program code as it is (no compilation). Their code will be checked for errors and then they will be asked to go back to the room where round 1 was conducted. If there are 3+ errors, they will be given a 20 sec penalty i.e. they will be asked to wait. The team which reaches the room back first will be the Winner..

Creatia

Event Details.

This event is meant to test the students on their spontaneity creativeness and competiveness. Each team will be given a random object or a property (image or name) and they have to list alternative best 4-5 uses of it. Each team will then be paired up with another team. Then each team gives a problem statement to the other team based on the uses listed by them. This round would be judged on how much concise, precise and challenging the problem statement is. In the next round, each team will have to give a solution to the problem statement. The team at the end may showcase their listed uses practically if they wish to.

CodeRiots

Event Details.

1.We will take any platform, say Greenfoot or Unity ( for Greenfoot, Java is required, for Unity, C# is required. We can decide on the platform depending on what language we want the participant to code.) 2. The games assets and environment will be preloaded. 3. The only thing left for the game to work will be coding. 4. Let's say the game will require 15 steps to build. 5. For each step, we will provide the participant four options. 6. The participant has to choose the right option to proceed to the next step. 7. A player will have a limited number of tries (say 3). 8. If he chooses the wrong answer three times, he loses and will have to quit and take part again and start building from the start. 9. If he successfully completes the required 15 steps, we will have an additional step. 10. In this additional step, the participant will not be provided any options. He will have to do something by his own to add some sort of feature to the game. 11. If he successfully completes the additional step, he will be given a cash prize and a certificate. 12. If he completes the basic steps and is unable to complete the additional step, he will be given a certificate.
